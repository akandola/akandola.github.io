Neuroscience Research: Past Projects & Lab Involvement

Discovering the role of PVT in motivated behavior (Summer 2016)

Working at Stanford University's Biology Department in Professor Xiaoke Chen's Lab; developed experiments to validate the role of the paraventricular thalamus in Pavlovian learning. Used MatLab scripts to analyze and visualize data.

Investigating the role of Prefrontal Cortex Circuits in Context-Dependent Attention (Summer 2015)

Worked in Professor Michael Halassa's Lab at NYU Langone (he's now at Tufts); Used Solidworks and 3D printers to build behavioral training systems, and used Arduino and MatLab scripts to carry out an experiment studying context-dependent color segregation in mice.

Computational Analysis of Neuronal Chromatin and Nuclear PARP-1/PAR Expression Provides Novel Marker for Detecting Learning-Associated Changes in Mice (2013-2015)

Worked in Dr. Ivan Hernandez' lab at SUNY Downstate; Created a program using NetLogo to identify memory encoding neurons within a network by quantifying confocal images of hippocampal neurons stained for chromatin, the enzyme PARP-1, and PAR. We identified PARP-1 as novel marker for detecting learning associated changes in mice and the program is still used in Dr. Hernandez' lab. My work was encapsualted in a research paper and the 2nd Grand Award in the Animal Sciences Category at the 2015 International Science and Engineering Fair.
